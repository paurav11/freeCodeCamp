Narendra Modi

Prime Minister of India

Here's the timeline of Narendra Modi:

  • 1950-Born to Hiraba and Damodardas Modi in Vadnagar in the state of Gujarat (Mehsana district).
  • 1965-India-Pakistan War Breaks Out. Narendra Modi serves tea at Mehsana Railway Station to Jawans.
  • 1967-Leaves home and travels across India including the Himalayas, Rishikesh, Ramakrishna Mission,…
  • 1972-Joins the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh. Takes a conscious decision to devote his entire life.
  • 1975-Emergency is imposed. Narendra Modi gets fully involved in anti-Emergency struggle.
  • 1980-The Bharatiya Janata Party is born.
  • 1987-Joins BJP as Organisation Secretary of Gujarat Unit.
  • 1990-BJP wins 67 seats in Gujarat Vidhan Sabha Elections and joins Chimanbhai Patel’s government.
  • 1995-BJP secures majority in Gujarat Assembly Elections, winning 121 seats.
  • 1995-Moves to Delhi as National Secretary. Given charge of various North Indian states Haryana,…
  • 1998-Becomes National General Secretary (Organisation), a prestigious Party post held by stalwarts.
  • 1999-BJP secures consecutive victories in Lok Sabha elections under Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee.
  • 2001-Sworn in as the Chief Minister of Gujarat.
  • 2002-Leads BJP to record win in 2002 Gujarat Assembly Elections.
  • 2007-Becomes longest serving CM of Gujarat.
  • 2007-BJP wins 2007 Gujarat Assembly Elections. Narendra Modi sworn-in as CM for 3rd time.
  • 2012-Leads BJP to yet another victory in the Gujarat Assembly Polls. Becomes CM for 4th time.
  • 2013-Named BJP’s Campaign Committee Chief for 2014 Lok Sabha Elections.
  • 2013-BJP declares Narendra Modi as the PM candidate of the Party for 2014 Lok Sabha Elections.
  • 2013-Foundation Stone for Statue of Unity laid by Shri LK Advani ji and Shri Narendra Modi.
  • 2014-Historic victory for NDA in 2014 Elections. Narendra Modi Tweets, "India has won! भारत की विजय।
  • 2014-Unanimously elected as Leader of BJP Parliamentary Party.
  • 2014-Sworn in as Prime Minister of India.
  • 2019-Takes oath as the Prime Minister of India.
